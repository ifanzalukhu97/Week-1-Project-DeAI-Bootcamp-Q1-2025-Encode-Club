## Recipe Request

### Input

```
What is the recipe for Salmon Pepes?
```

### Answer

```
Ah, Salmon Pepes! A dish that captures the spirit of Indonesia, embracing the earth and sea in its fragrant embrace. Let us prepare this delightful dish together. Here is the recipe for Salmon Pepes:

### Ingredients:
- 500 grams of fresh salmon fillet
- 3 tablespoons of lime juice
- 1 teaspoon of salt
- 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
- 2-3 lime leaves (finely shredded)
- 2-3 Indonesian bay leaves (daun salam)
- 2 stalks of lemongrass (white part only, finely chopped)
- 4-5 Thai bird's eye chilies (adjust to your spice preference, finely chopped)
- 5 cloves of garlic (minced)
- 2 shallots (sliced)
- 1 inch piece of ginger (grated)
- 1-2 teaspoons of sugar (to balance the flavors)
- 4-6 banana leaves (to wrap the salmon)

### Instructions:

1. **Marinate the Salmon:**
   - In a bowl, mix the fresh salmon fillet with lime juice, salt, and a bit of vegetable oil. Let it marinate for about 15-20 minutes. This will infuse the fish with flavor and keep it tender.

2. **Prepare the Spice Mixture:**
   - In a pan, heat the remaining vegetable oil over medium heat.
   - Add the minced garlic, sliced shallots, and finely chopped lemongrass. Sauté until fragrant and the shallots are slightly soft (about 2-3 minutes).
   - Stir in the chopped chilies, shredded lime leaves, and Indonesian bay leaves. Cook for another minute.
   - Finally, add ginger and sugar to balance the heat, allowing it to cook for 1 more minute. Set aside.

3. **Assemble the Pepes:**
   - Take a banana leaf and cut it into rectangles, about 30 cm x 20 cm. Soften the banana leaves by briefly passing them over a flame or steaming them, making them more pliable.
   - Place a piece of salmon in the center of each banana leaf rectangle. Top it with the spice mixture from the pan.
   - Fold the banana leaves over the salmon to securely wrap it, creating a parcel. You can use toothpicks or kitchen twine to hold them together if needed.

4. **Cook the Salmon:**
   - You can either steam or grill the wrapped salmon parcels. For steaming, place them in a steamer basket and cook for about 20-25 minutes. If grilling, place them on the grill for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally.

5. **Serve:**
   - Carefully unwrap the banana leaves and serve the Salmon Pepes hot with steamed rice and a side of your favorite sambal for an extra kick of flavor.

Enjoy this dish, my friend! It is a creation that not only warms the belly but also nourishes the soul. If you have any further questions or need more assistance, feel free to ask. Happy cooking!
```

## Recipe Critique

**Ingredients:**
- 400g fresh salmon, cut into 4 pieces
- 4 banana leaves (approx. 20x20 cm each), softened

- Fresh basil leaves (daun kemangi), handful

**Spice Paste (Bumbu Halus):**
- 5 shallots
- 4 garlic cloves
- 5 red chilies (cabe merah keriting), adjust to taste
- 3 bird's eye chilies (optional, for extra heat)
- 3 candlenuts (kemiri)
- 2 cm ginger
- 2 cm turmeric (kunyit)
- 1 tsp sugar
- ½ tsp ground white pepper

**Instructions:**
1. **Prepare Spice Paste:**  
   Blend all spice paste ingredients into a smooth paste. Set aside.

2. **Marinate Fish:**  
   Rub the salmon pieces generously with the spice paste and sprinkle with a little salt. Let it marinate for 15-20 minutes.

3. **Wrap the Pepes:**  
   On each banana leaf, place a piece of salmon. Add lemongrass, bay leaf, and basil leaves on top. Fold and secure the banana leaf with toothpicks.

4. **Cook the Pepes:**  
   Steam the wrapped salmon over medium heat for about 20-25 minutes until cooked through.

5. **Grill (Optional but recommended):**  
   Briefly grill each pepes packet over charcoal or a grill pan for a few minutes on each side for a delicious smoky flavor.

6. **Serve:**
